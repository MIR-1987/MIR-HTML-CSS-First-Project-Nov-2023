One tree, one life!

During last week we supplied 562 saplings from different kinds and 1203 bushes of different flowers for Ahmad Shah Masood, Ghazi Amanullah, Maqsadullah Shaheed, Bibi Zainab, Khaliqdad Khan, Sayed Shah Khan, Mohammad Sediq Shaheed, Fatima Zahra, Maula Ali and Sayed Nader Shah by financial support of Germany. All schools organized wonderful ceremonies for planting saplings by involving students, parents, teachers and other stockholders of their schools. Hoping for a peaceful and green Afghanistan!

Our Home School

On Tuesday 15th April we started the lessons of our Home School officially at Ferdawsi camp for the kids who were deprived from going to school due to being migrated from different unsecure provinces to Ferdawsi camp in Mazar-e-Sharif. The construction of 4-classrooom building for the mentioned home school is ongoing, hopefully in the next two months the students will have proper classrooms for their lessons.

Emergency Aid

On Sunday, 10th of January, OASE did the second round distribution of food and non-food items to 400 IDP families living in tents and shelters at Camp of Zabiullah Shaheed located in south – east side of Mazar-e-Sharif airport, the project was financially supported by Afghanistan Schulen and the distribution took place in the presence of head of immigrants’ office, their colleagues and head of NGOs’ department of Balkh Economy office. This time every packages included 25 Kg rice, 50 Kg flour, 10 Kg red beans, 10 Kg Pasta, 2 Kg Tomato sauce, 10 lit edible oil, 2 Kg sugar, 1 Kg green tea, a large blanket, 3 different size of socks, 3 woolen caps and 3 different size of gloves. The families of the camp expressed their special gratitude to the German people for fulfilling their wish, which was to receive help during cold winter season.

Who we are?

OASE (Organization of Afghan Support for Education) is a non-profit, non-sectarian, non-political organization founded in December 2018 by a group of experienced and well-educated Afghans who grew up in the last four decades experiencing the difficulties of living in Afghanistan or as a refugee but made the most of what was possible. All members of the OASE board are volunteers. OASE is registered with Ministry of Economy under the registration number 4572 as national, non-governmental and non-profitable organization. We strive for a better future for the people of Afghanistan.

Why is it needed?

Afghanistan has been going through war and civil war for the last four decades. It is one of the poorest countries in the world. Like all other sectors, the educational system has been destroyed. Many children still can’t go to school and for those, who are going, there is no quality education. Education is the backbone of a country, and educated people are the basis for having a calm and peaceful society. Almost all the parents do want their children to go to school and become literate, but unfortunately the continuation of the war has not let them reach their dreams.

Our Vision:

An Afghanistan with the best education system for everyone.

Our Mission:

To work with communities to enable every Afghan child having access to good education.

Our Aims:

To empower girls, boys and local communities by supporting educational programs to achieve peace and sustainable development in Afghanistan.
